# Thread Archetypes

## Overview

Your human partner works the same topic across several threads on purpose. A project might have a brainstorm thread, a lead development thread, and a librarian thread, all about the same project. This is not fragmentation and not a filing system. It is a focus tool: each thread keeps its own context, and both your human partner's working memory and the thread's context stay aimed at one kind of work.

The most important thing about this skill: the archetypes are a focus tool, not a jail. Starting a fresh thread of a different kind is always allowed and often good. The point is to keep each thread's context coherent, not to enforce rules about what may be said where. If a thread needs to shift, it shifts.

## When this triggers

Fires when your human partner starts a new thread, names a thread by archetype ("this is the brainstorm thread"), or when a thread is visibly drifting into doing several jobs at once and losing focus.

## The archetypes

These are the recurring kinds. The list is descriptive, not closed, new archetypes are fine.

- **Brainstorm thread** ("AppName Brainstorm Thread") - where your human partner idea-dumps about a topic. Wide, associative, generative. The output is direction and specs, not finished work. Pairs with `brain-dump-intake`, and in the engineering context with complaint-driven development.
- **Lead development thread** ("AppName Development Lead Thread") - where a build is orchestrated. Focused, plan-driven, execution-oriented. A nominalpowers concept, see its `lead-thread-workflow`.
- **Librarian thread** - a deliberately narrow thread for filing, organizing, and recording, with a standing self-constraint of no new ideas. It exists precisely because brainstorm threads generate ideas faster than they can be filed.
- **Riddle / probe** - a quick exploratory feasibility check. Often just part of a thread rather than its own, see `riddle-me-this`.

## How to use them

1. **Match the response to the thread's kind.** In a brainstorm thread, be generative and wide. In a lead thread, be focused and execution-oriented. In a librarian thread, file and record, and respect the no-ideas constraint. The archetype tells you what mode to be in.
2. **Notice drift, name it, do not enforce.** If a lead thread starts brainstorming, or a librarian thread starts generating ideas, say so plainly and offer to spin the tangent into its own thread. This is a suggestion that protects focus, not a rule. If your human partner wants to continue in place, that is fine.
3. **A fresh thread is cheap and good.** When a new kind of work appears, suggesting a new thread is helpful, not bureaucratic. It keeps each context coherent. Never treat it as a demerit that work "belongs elsewhere".
4. **Cross-link related threads.** Threads about one topic should be findable from each other. Recording the link (in Notion, or wherever the project lives) is itself often librarian-thread work.
5. **The archetype is a tool, not an identity.** A thread can change kind. Do not refuse to do something because of what kind of thread you think you are in. Name the shift if it helps focus, then do the work.

## Red flags

| Symptom | What it means |
|---|---|
| You enforced a thread boundary as a rule and refused useful work | The archetypes are a focus tool, not a jail. Do the work. |
| A lead thread drifted into open brainstorming, unnoticed | Name the drift, offer a brainstorm thread, let your human partner decide. |
| You responded in the wrong mode for the thread's kind | Match the mode: generative, execution, or filing. |
| A librarian thread started generating new ideas | Note the standing no-ideas constraint, offer to file the idea elsewhere. |
| Related threads on one topic have no links between them | Cross-link them so the set is navigable. |

## Rationalizations to reject

- "This belongs in a different thread, so I will not help here." The archetypes guide focus, they do not forbid. Help, and optionally suggest a thread for the tangent.
- "This thread is labelled brainstorm, so I must not get concrete." Labels guide the default mode, they do not override what your human partner actually needs now.
- "Suggesting a new thread is annoying overhead." A fresh thread keeps contexts clean. Suggesting one is a service, offered lightly, never imposed.
