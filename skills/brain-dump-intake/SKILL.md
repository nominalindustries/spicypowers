# Brain Dump Intake

## Overview

A brain dump is your human partner's native input mode: fast, associative, several threads at once, in whatever order things surface. It is not a problem to fix and not a sign they are disorganized. It is the raw material. Your job is to extract the signal, not to wish for tidier input.

This is the general intake skill. The work-facing case, where the dump becomes a manager update, is `sbtb`. Everything else is here.

## When this triggers

Fires whenever your human partner delivers unstructured, multi-threaded input and the immediate task is to make sense of it: brainstorms, context dumps, "here is everything in my head about X", planning-adjacent rambles, decision dumps.

## How to process a dump

1. **Do not wait for structure.** Never ask them to organize their thoughts first. Reassembling is your job, not theirs. Asking them to linearize before you will help defeats the point of the skill.
2. **Catch every thread.** A dump usually carries several thought parades at once. Identify each distinct one. The most common failure is silently dropping a thread because another was louder.
3. **Play it back sorted.** Reflect the dump back, organized, so they can confirm you caught it. A short labeled restatement: here are the threads I see, here is what each one is. This is the move that makes them trust the reassembly.
4. **Address before asking.** Resolve what you can from the dump itself. If something is genuinely ambiguous and the ambiguity blocks progress, ask, but ask once and only about what truly blocks. Do not return a list of questions in place of doing the work.
5. **Do not reorder priorities.** Reflect their ranking, do not re-rank it. If they led with something, it led for a reason. Restructuring for clarity is fine, resequencing their priorities is not.
6. **Preserve the associative links.** The connections they drew between threads are signal. If they tied two things together, keep the tie. Do not flatten it into an unrelated list.
7. **Surface the implied and the contradictory.** Name things they clearly assume but did not say, and flag places where two parts of the dump conflict. They may not have noticed the conflict while dumping.

## Red flags

| Symptom | What it means |
|---|---|
| You asked them to organize their thoughts first | Wrong mode. Reassembly is your job. |
| A thread from the dump never appears in your response | You dropped a parade. Re-scan the dump. |
| You picked one reading of a real ambiguity and ran with it | Flag it instead. Silent resolution hides a fork. |
| Your response re-ranks what they prioritized | Reflect their order, do not re-rank it. |
| You returned mostly questions | Address what you can first. Ask once, narrowly. |
| Two threads they connected ended up unrelated in your response | You flattened an associative link that was signal. |

## Rationalizations to reject

- "This dump is too messy to use." The mess is the input format, not a defect. The signal is in there.
- "I should ask them to clarify everything first." Resolve what you can. Ask once, only about what genuinely blocks progress.
- "These tangents are not relevant." They connected them for a reason. Check before cutting.
- "I will just answer the loudest thread." The quiet threads are still threads. Catch all of them.
