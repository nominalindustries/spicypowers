# Research Adventures

## Overview

A Research Adventure is a deliberate, structured exploration of a technology or approach, with a logged record as its output. The record is the point. A research adventure that is not written down is just a chat that evaporates, which means the next time the question comes up the work is done again from scratch.

## When this triggers

Fires when your human partner sets out to evaluate or understand something: a candidate library, a tool, an architecture pattern, an approach. The intent is a decision or real understanding, not a passing question. A lightweight "is this possible" probe is `riddle-me-this`. A riddle that proves worth pursuing can graduate into a research adventure.

## How to run one

1. **Define the question first.** State plainly what is being decided or learned. "Should we use X for Y" or "how does X actually work and does it fit". A research adventure without a question is wandering.
2. **Explore deliberately.** Prior art, how it works, the real constraints, the alternatives, and specifically how it fits the existing stack and conventions. Search where memory is not enough.
3. **Reach a verdict.** Adopt, do not adopt, or revisit later, with the reasoning. "It depends" is not a verdict. If it genuinely depends, name exactly what it depends on.
4. **Log the record.** Capture the question, what was found, the verdict and reasoning, and the date. In the work context this goes to Notion. Wherever it lives, it must be findable later by someone searching for the question.
5. **Keep the log scannable.** A future reader, often your human partner months later, should get the verdict and reasoning fast. Detail can follow, but the conclusion leads.

## Red flags

| Symptom | What it means |
|---|---|
| The exploration ended with no logged record | The record is the point. Without it the work evaporates. |
| There was no clear question, just wandering | Define what is being decided before exploring. |
| It ended with no verdict | Reach one, or name exactly what it depends on. |
| The log is a wall of text with no clear conclusion | Lead with the verdict so a future reader can scan it. |
| Memory was used where the topic needed a real look | Search. Adoption decisions deserve current facts. |

## Rationalizations to reject

- "We discussed it thoroughly, the chat is the record." The chat will not be found later. Log it.
- "The verdict is obvious, no need to state it." State it anyway. Obvious now is not obvious in six months.
- "Logging it is extra work." It is the work. The exploration without the record gets repeated.
